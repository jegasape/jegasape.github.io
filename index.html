About

Over the course of 12 years, I have developed systems that operate well when they are working and have problems when they don't, with scalable architecture and automated processes giving people back their time. My primary programming languages are C#, TypeScript, Go and Python, but writing code that is working for this moment is not my sole craft. I also want to write code that will continue to work after I've moved on to another opportunity.

I don't think of deployment methods as an afterthought, but as part of my thought process. Both Docker and Kubernetes can be used to accomplish the goals of "quickly" and "with certainty," and I believe they do not need to be opposed. I am concerned when projects are completed about whether or not the maintainers will have the skills to do so. Any system that relies on one person's capability to maintain it is a risk.

I will support my coworkers and invest time in developing them through mentoring and justifying my decision-making. I believe the greatest value comes from creating a cohesive team that shares the same understanding of the solution they collectively developed. I am most successful working through difficult problems with significant ramifications, alongside other serious-minded people in pursuit of the best solution.
